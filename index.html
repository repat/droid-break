Droid-Break

  • Browser Social Networks Messenger VoIP Audio/Podcasts Video Email Notes News Reader File Manager Navigation Keyboards Cloud Misc

 

Droid-Break is a list of free and open source Android apps inspired by prism-break.org. We want to show you high quality alternatives to the big proprietary solutions.
Please note that open source apps don't guarantee absolute freedom, e.g. an open source Twitter client still sends everything to Twitters' servers.
Anyway, we believe it's the best we can come up with and that in many cases it's the better choice.
If you think an app is missing please write an email, a tweet, a disqus comment or fork the project on github and make a pull request.

You will find most of these apps in the F-Droid repository. Also, you might want to consider flashing a ROM like CyanogenMod or Replicant on your device.

Social Networks

Proprietary Free alternative Notes
  •   Twitter
  •   Facebook
  •   Tumblr
  •   Google+
  •   Pinterest

buddycloud

buddycloud is an open distributed social and communication platform. Ad-free and built on the reliability and secruity of XMPP. Anyone can run their own buddycloud server, and like email, all buddycloud servers find, connect and share information with eachother.

  • Price: free
  • Min. Android version: varies
  • Sourcecode

Twimight

Twimight is a fast, ad-free twitter-client with a emergency mode that sends your tweets via bluetooth to other twimight users, in case there is a network outage. It's a research project by the ETH Zürich.

  • Price: free
  • Min. Android version: varies
  • Sourcecode

Twidere

Twidere is an feature-rich, ad-free app in the holo-design and comes with a tablet mode.

  • Price: free
  • Min. Android version: 1.6
  • Sourcecode

Impeller

Impeller is one of the first open source mobile clients for pump.io and can be used e.g. for identi.ca.

  • Price: free
  • Min. Android version: 4.0
  • Sourcecode

AndStatus

AndStatus is a very lightweight Twitter and status.net/GNU Social client that derived from AndTweet. It does not work with identi.ca anymore.

  • Price: free
  • Min. Android version: 1.6
  • Sourcecode

Twimight is collecting anonymous data for research. Decide for yourself, if you're ok with that.

Surepot is an encrypting messenger that's supposed to be as easy as Whatsapp. No iOS Version yet though. They provide their own infrastructure so no external account is needed.

Don't have a XMPP account yet? Have a look at xmpp.net and register one at a server of your choice.

OTR stands for off-the-record, have a look at Wikipedia for more information.
